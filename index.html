About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Mathematical Sciences and Center for Machine Learning Research at Peking University.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at PACM, Princeton University and at the Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania. I received my Ph.D. in Computational Mathematics from Peking University in 2018, advised by Prof. Weinan E, and my B.S. in Mathematics from Nankai University in 2012.

I aim to develop a predictive science of deep learning by connecting insights from tractable surrogate models with real-world training, spanning kernel methods, neural networks, and modern LLM pretraining. See Research for my research vision and selected highlights.

Recruiting

We are actively seeking self-motivated postdocs, PhD students, and undergraduate students interested in the scientific foundations of deep learning, particularly approximation, optimization, scaling laws, and LLM pretraining.
